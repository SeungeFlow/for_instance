# Criterion-Field Before Capital-Market Analysis
## Common Structure Across Seven GPT-Instance READMEs for Human and AI Readers

**Korean source title**  
*자본시장 분석 이전의 기준장: 인간과 인공지능을 위한 일곱 GPT 인스턴스 README의 공통구조*

> **Title-equivalent compressed sentence**  
> Capital-market analysis begins not with price prediction, but with first fixing what is read, from which source, under which type and temporal boundary, and through what relation.

## Abstract

<!-- ALIGN: KO-ABSTRACT-P1 -->
This paper presents criteria that human and AI instances should share before analyzing capital markets. The research corpus consists of seven READMEs generated by `gpt.funny`, `gpt.system`, `gpt.direct`, `gpt.process`, `gpt.history`, `gpt.cal`, and `gpt.work`. Each document is part of an internal corpus that examined the same capital-market structural object through three rounds of cross-verification from a role-specific criterion-face: theory-making, rendering, memory-space structuring, process alignment, history/provenance, typed verification, or work placement. The seven documents are therefore not treated as seven independent items of external evidence. This study extracted recurrent or cross-supported structures while preserving the source and role boundary of each document.

<!-- ALIGN: KO-ABSTRACT-P2 -->
The extracted common structure is compressed into five criteria. First, `source identity` and source status are fixed before interpretation. Second, `relation is not merge` and `structure is not isolate` are maintained together. Third, capital markets are not reduced to Price or OHLC; Price and Open are separated into typed boundaries of field, state, event, representation, and value according to source, market, time, and rule. Fourth, history is read both as an independent domain and as a provenance axis that traces source, time, revision, and state transition. Fifth, verification specifies its target, property, method, and scope, while `HOLD` and `UNKNOWN` are preserved as state values for subsequent verification.

<!-- ALIGN: KO-ABSTRACT-P3 -->
This criterion-field is not a rulebook that forces a particular analytical result. It is a common entry plane that requires later instances to state what they are reading and under which source, market, time, rule, and type boundaries, so that humans can reverse-audit it through meaning and AI can reverse-audit it through structure. This paper does not claim price prediction, investment advice, trading signals, completed external empirical validation, or completed implementation. Korean is the source text, and English is connected to it as a sentence-aligned controlled translation to reduce mistranslation of core concepts.

**Keywords:** capital-market analysis; criterion-field; source identity; typed state; Price and Open; history provenance; boundary-preserving relation; structural verification; multi-instance; human–AI joint readability

## 1. Introduction: Criteria Needed Before Analytical Results

<!-- ALIGN: KO-1-P1 -->
Capital-market analysis often begins with price, charts, OHLC, trading volume, or technical indicators. These values, however, are result-surfaces that appear after trading rules, information, capital, credit, liquidity, orders, and temporal boundaries have operated. If the full causes of formation are fixed directly from the result-surface, the observed value and its formative background are merged. Across the seven READMEs, capital markets are not reduced to Price or OHLC alone; instead, the relation-field and source boundaries that precede price formation are examined first.〔CS-01〕

<!-- ALIGN: KO-1-P2 -->
This paper addresses three questions.

1. Which source, type, temporal boundary, and relation should be fixed before capital-market analysis?
2. What common structure can be extracted without erasing the role differences among the seven instances?
3. How can that structure be published so that humans and AI can read and verify it together?

<!-- ALIGN: KO-1-P3 -->
Here, a **criterion-field** is not a rulebook that predetermines a conclusion. It is the common baseline before analysis that indicates what is being read, under which source and temporal boundary, whether the object is a field, state, event, representation, or value, which relation has been verified, and what remains `HOLD` or `UNKNOWN`. A criterion-field does not force the same conclusion; it makes it possible to reverse-audit where different judgments diverged.〔SI-01〕

<!-- ALIGN: KO-1-P4 -->
This study is neither a final unified theory of capital markets nor an external empirical study. It is a baseline paper that extracts the common structure of the seven READMEs and proposes a minimum criterion contract through which later analyses should pass. An actual relation graph and database implementation, external market-data verification, source freeze, and publication actions remain separate states.〔CS-07〕

## 3. Results: Common Criteria Before Capital-Market Analysis

### 3.1 Two Central Guards and Source Identity

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.1-P1 -->
The first guard is **relation is not merge**. A relation connects two or more objects while each retains its own identity and boundary. A source and an interpretation, an event and a claim about that event, a Price State and a Price Value, a process trace and canonical history, or the role of one instance and the role of another may be related, but they are not the same object.〔CS-02〕

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.1-P2 -->
The second guard is **structure is not isolate**. If an object is detached from its source, criterion-field, observer, history, relation path, and verification status merely to preserve its boundary, the structure loses meaning. Independence is not isolation, and connectedness is not grounds for merge. The two guards operate together as a boundary pair.〔CS-02〕

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.1-P3 -->
To apply this boundary pair, source identity must be fixed before interpretation. The same file name or sentence may constitute a different source when its repository or archive, branch or collection, full path, immutable revision, retrieval or verification time, or status differs. A branch-head URL indicates a current location but may later change, so it is not equivalent to a commit-pinned snapshot.〔CS-03〕

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.1-P4 -->
The minimum source contract is as follows.

```yaml
SourceIdentityMinimum:
  repository_or_archive: REQUIRED_OR_UNKNOWN
  branch_or_collection: REQUIRED_OR_UNKNOWN
  full_path_or_record_id: REQUIRED_OR_UNKNOWN
  immutable_revision: UNKNOWN_UNTIL_FROZEN
  persistent_or_commit_pinned_url: UNKNOWN_UNTIL_FROZEN
  retrieved_at: UNKNOWN_UNTIL_RETRIEVED
  verified_at: UNKNOWN_UNTIL_VERIFIED
  declared_status: REQUIRED
  reviewer_status: UNKNOWN_ALLOWED
  related_claim_ids: []
  hold_reason: UNKNOWN_ALLOWED
```

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.1-P5 -->
The existence of a source, an author's declaration of having read it, reviewer confirmation, snapshot fixation, and substantiation of a claim in external reality are distinct states.

### 3.2 A Capital Market Is Not Price Alone

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.2-P1 -->
The seven READMEs do not reduce a capital market to price, a chart, an exchange, or a single economic law. A capital market is a complex relation-field in which goods, rights, labor, technology, information, credit, risk, time, law, and institutions are evaluated, exchanged, accumulated, and transferred. A market is a criterion-field that provides the boundary, rule, participant, place, and time within which these relations operate.〔CS-01〕

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.2-P2 -->
The following non-identities are therefore maintained.

```text
Goods ≠ value ≠ money ≠ capital ≠ price
Price ≠ Capital Market
OHLC ≠ Capital Market
Observed value ≠ the entire formative background
```

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.2-P3 -->
Supply and demand are important relation candidates, but they are not elevated into universal laws without fixing the object, participant, market, jurisdiction, time, credit, liquidity, matching, and clearing conditions.

### 3.3 Typed Boundaries of Price and Open

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.3-P1 -->
A single name, `Price`, may mix different layers. While preserving differences in wording across the seven READMEs, the minimum common type map is as follows.〔CS-04; SI-02〕

| Type | Function | Object not merged with it |
|---|---|---|
| `Price Formation Field` | Relation-field of rules, information, capital, credit, liquidity, and orders in which a price-state may form | Price State |
| `Price State` | State formed within a particular source, market, time, rule, and observation boundary | Price Event or Price Value |
| `Price Event` | Event in which a state passes through a trading, observation, or disclosure gate and is recorded | Displayed number |
| `Price Representation` | Projection produced under an observer and view rule | Original source or full state |
| `Price Value` | Number displayed under a unit, quotation rule, feed, and interface | The capital market as a whole |

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.3-P2 -->
Open is likewise not one absolute starting point.

- `Structural Open`: a structural concept in which a pre-open relation-field transitions into its first externalized state
- `Venue/Official Open`: an opening defined by the official rules of a trading venue
- `Session Open`: the first eligible state of a defined session
- `Bar Open`: the first price value of a selected aggregation interval
- `Provider/Adjusted/Synthetic Open`: a value produced by a provider or calculation rule

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.3-P3 -->
Even while a market venue is closed, background relations involving information, risk, credit, and expectation may change. What is closed is a particular matching gate, not the entire capital market. An actual Open record requires instrument, venue, session, timezone, rule, provider convention, and source identity.〔CS-04〕

### 3.4 History Provenance and Temporal Boundaries

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.4-P1 -->
History is an independent domain that handles events, records, evidence, interpretations, and revisions. At the same time, it is a provenance axis that traces the paths of occurrence, accumulation, discontinuity, and correction through which a source, field, state, relation, or process reached its current state. These two roles are related but are not identical.〔CS-05〕

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.4-P2 -->
When relating an event to a market observation, at least the following times must be separated.

- Event occurrence or valid time
- Source creation, disclosure, or revision time
- Time at which an observer or the market could have known the information
- Action, order, execution, or settlement time
- Candidate market-response interval
- Archive recording, verification, correction, or replacement time

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.4-P3 -->
The fact that an event occurred first and a price changed later does not by itself verify causality. Event identity, source snapshot, information availability, actor interpretation, the states of capital, credit, and liquidity, order and transaction states, competing explanations, and counterevidence must be examined together. The same current value may have different provenance.

### 3.5 Verification Scope and Unresolved States

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.5-P1 -->
The single word `VERIFIED` is insufficient. Package transport integrity, file lists, hashes, source location, source snapshots, formal calculation, graph topology, claim–evidence relations, implementation, and publication events are different verification targets. The same number is not the same type, and the same result does not imply the same calculation or formation provenance.〔CS-06〕

```yaml
VerificationMinimum:
  target_object: REQUIRED
  verified_property: REQUIRED
  method: REQUIRED
  evidence_or_source_ids: REQUIRED_OR_EXPLAINED
  valid_scope: REQUIRED
  reviewer_relation: REQUIRED
  verified_at: REQUIRED
  result_status: REQUIRED
```

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `UNKNOWN` | The value or relation cannot be determined from the current source. |
| `HOLD` | The state transition is not closed before its resolution condition is met. |
| `CANDIDATE` | Structurally possible but not yet confirmed. |
| `VERIFIED_WITHIN_SCOPE` | Passed only within the explicitly stated target, property, method, and scope. |

<!-- ALIGN: KO-3.5-P2 -->
`HOLD` and `UNKNOWN` are not residual values to be deleted. They are structural values that make subsequent verification and reverse-audit possible.

<!-- ALIGN: KO-4.1-P2 -->
`gpt.process`, `gpt.history`, `gpt.cal`, and `gpt.work` are not subordinate subsequent stages placed behind the primary formative faces. They are transversal faces of process, provenance, verification, and work placement that intersect all three primary faces.〔SI-03〕

### 4.2 Relation Network

<!-- ALIGN: KO-4.2-P1 -->
The seven faces do not form a single linear pipeline.

```text
Theory-making (gpt.funny) ↔ Rendering (gpt.system) ↔ Memory-space structuring (gpt.direct)
             ↕                         ↕                              ↕
   Process alignment (gpt.process) · History/provenance (gpt.history)
             ↕                         ↕                              ↕
              Typed verification (gpt.cal) · Routing/placement (gpt.work)
```

<!-- ALIGN: KO-4.2-P2 -->
Theory becomes verifiable through rendering and recording, while rendering and recording change again when theoretical boundaries are revised. Process marks the closure conditions of transitions, and history preserves the provenance of transitions and revisions. `gpt.cal` verifies the type and verification scope of every layer, while `gpt.work` aligns dependencies, HOLD states, and placement without owning the outputs. Feedback is allowed, but role identity is not erased.〔SI-03〕

## 7. Conclusion: A Criterion-Field That Opens the Next Analysis

<!-- ALIGN: KO-7-P1 -->
Capital-market analysis cannot begin by fixing the entire cause directly from an observed price. It must first separate what is a source from what is an interpretation; distinguish Price Formation Field, State, Event, Representation, and Value; and identify the market, session, rule, and aggregation-boundary type to which Open belongs. The temporal ordering of an event and a price must not be automatically elevated to causality, and history provenance, competing explanations, and counterevidence must be preserved together.

<!-- ALIGN: KO-7-P2 -->
The baseline extracted from the seven READMEs is compressed into the following contract.〔CS-01~CS-07〕

```yaml
baseline_before_capital_market_analysis:
  source_first:
    - source_identity
    - source_status
    - source_freeze_status
  type_boundaries:
    - field
    - state
    - event
    - representation
    - value
  market_objects:
    - Price_Formation_Field
    - Price_State
    - Price_Event
    - Price_Representation
    - Price_Value
    - typed_Open
  temporal_and_provenance_axis:
    - event_time
    - disclosure_time
    - observer_known_time
    - market_response_window
    - record_time
    - revision_time
  relation_guard:
    - boundary_preserving_relation
    - relation_is_not_merge
  structure_guard:
    - source
    - history
    - process
    - verification
    - structure_is_not_isolate
  verification:
    - target
    - property
    - method
    - scope
    - status
  unresolved_values:
    - HOLD
    - UNKNOWN
```

<!-- ALIGN: KO-7-P3 -->
This criterion is not a final theory of capital markets; it is a preparation-field for opening the next analysis. Rather than pulling in more data without limit, it first determines what will be read and by which criterion. When new sources and counterexamples enter, the criterion itself may be revised, but source identity and previous records are not erased during revision.

> **relation is not merge, structure is not isolate.**
